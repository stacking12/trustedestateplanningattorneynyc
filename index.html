Do I need a will if I already have a trust?

Yes. Even with a trust in place, a "pour-over will" is an essential companion document. It ensures any assets not explicitly transferred into your trust during your lifetime are captured and directed to the trust upon your passing. Our trust attorneys in Manhattan will structure both documents to work seamlessly together, closing any gaps in your estate plan.

How early should I start Medicaid planning in New York?

The sooner, the better. New York Medicaid has a five-year look-back period for nursing home care, meaning asset transfers made within five years of applying can affect your eligibility. Starting early with a qualified NYC Medicaid Planning Attorney allows you to structure your assets legally and protect your savings well in advance — avoiding costly mistakes and preserving more of your estate for your family.

What is the probate process in New York, and how long does it take?

Probate in New York is the court-supervised process of validating a will and overseeing the distribution of a deceased person's estate. It typically takes between 9 months and 2 years depending on the complexity of the estate, the presence of disputes, and the court's schedule. Morgan Legal Group's Probate Lawyer Manhattan NY team works to streamline this process, handle all filings on your behalf, and resolve any issues as efficiently as possible.
